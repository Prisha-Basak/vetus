FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Answers to some questions you might have



A thrift store, often called a secondhand store or a thrift shop, is a retail establishment that offers used, donated, or pre-owned goods at cheaper costs than those offered by standard retail establishments. Clothing, footwear, accessories, furniture, household goods, technology, books, toys, and other items can be included in this category. Charity groups, non-profits, or for-profit companies frequently run thrift stores.

The "slow movement" promotes clothes and apparel manufacture with consideration for people, the environment, and animals. Slow fashion is a component of sustainable fashion and a concept expressing the antithesis of rapid fashion.

Fashion Revolution Week is a global event that aims to raise awareness about the negative impact of the fashion industry on the environment and workers' rights. The event involves activities such as documentary screenings, clothing swaps, social media campaigns, and eco-friendly fashion shows. The goal is to educate people about sustainable fashion, encourage them to ask brands about the origin of their clothes, and promote alternatives to fast fashion.
